Father Bill's & MainSpring

For more than three decades, Father Bill’s & MainSpring (FBMS), has been a leading innovator of ending homelessness. Their mission is to end and prevent homelessness in Southern Massachusetts with programs that provide emergency and permanent housing and help people obtain skills, jobs, housing, and services. They help people who are struggling with homelessness, or are at risk of homelessness, to achieve self-sufficiency.

While they do not directly service those in recovery, they do have a “housing first” philosophy, meaning that having a safe place to stay is the foundation to success in other parts of life. Father Bill’s & MainSpring has a vital role in helping people in recovery in the local area, as people all too often end up homeless due to their addiction. That is why they are the perfect fit for our cause.

Our father, Scott Benoit, had also stayed at and received support from Father Bill’s and MainSpring a couple times during his struggle with addiction and homelessness. He even worked there for some time while he was in recovery. Father Bill’s & MainSpring was a big crutch when he needed support and a place to stay, and we are proud to be able to help such a life-changing organization.

If you would like any more information on Father Bill’s & MainSpring, please check out their website: helpfbms.org

Our Story

Our father, Scott Benoit, passed away in June 2017 due to another person’s addiction and actions while under the influence. While the story is devastating, we want to bring hope from the situation and use our story to benefit the well-being of those still fighting the battle against substance use and addiction.

Scott was an alcoholic. We witnessed his battle with his disease first-hand and know the struggles people suffering from addiction face. It’s a living nightmare, and unfortunately, people facing that fight do not always get the support they need. There is a stigma around being ‘an addict’ that implies that you are less than and hopeless. That is just not true. We know substance use is a chronic disorder, and just like any other chronic illness, can be treated with constant work and a lot of support.

That is why we started this 5K, to help raise awareness for those battling substance use disorder like our father did. Together, we can fight the stigma and turn our tragic story into someone else’s light at the end of the tunnel.

Join us again this year as we continue to fight the stigma of substance use and addiction.
